The Highlight Albums:

Depression Cherry by Beach House

Rating: 9.2/10; Genre: Dream Pop, Indie Rock, Shoe Gaze

  1. Beach House Playlist

Beach House has an entirely unique and classical sound. A sound that creates an out-of-body experience. Depression Cherry is Beach House's most popular album that features their most popular song, "Spaces Song." Beach House utilizes dreamy synth sounds to create a mystical and whimsical experience. Each sound heavenly blends into eachother on the album. This album holds a special place in my heart as this was my first introduction to Beach House, my favorite musical artist. My favorite songs off of this album are "Space Song," "Beyond Love," "PPP," "Days of Candy," and "10:37."

The Anthology by A Tribe Called Quest

Rating: 9.1/10; Genre: Hip Hop, Jazz Rap, Progressive Rap

This collection created a large impact in the Hip Hop community, influencing a lot of modern day Hip Hop/R&B artists. The album features A Tribe Called Quest's most popular songs. This collection possesses many catchy tunes. The lyrical flow of the songs on the album is entirely impressive. This album adresses themes of race, poverty, and relationships. My favorite songs off the album are "Bonita Applebum," "Check the Rhime," "Can I Kick It?," "Jazz," and "Electric Relaxation."

Channel Orange by Frank Ocean

Rating: 9.6/10; Genre: Hip Hop, Alternative R&B, Neo-Soul

Channel Orange my favorite album by Frank Ocean. Each song beautifully bleeds into eachother thus creating a brilliant album as a whole. Alongside the carefully crafted lyrical flow, Frank supports his lyrics with a unique sound ecosystem. This album adresses on topics of identity, romance, poverty, drugs, religion, and sexuality. My favorite songs off of the album are "Sweet Life," "Crack Rock," "Pyramids," "Monks," and "Bad Religion."
